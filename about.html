Our Story

Crafting furniture that becomes part of a family's story

The Beginning

Born from a love
of honest craft

Casa Liora began in a small workshop in Limuru ,Kiambu, in 2026. Founder Tracey Wangui had spent a decade working with European furniture houses before returning to Kenya with a singular vision: to create world-class designer furniture that speaks the language of the African landscape.

The name "Casa" Italian for home reflects our belief that furniture is not merely functional. It is the architecture of memory, the backdrop of your most intimate moments.

"Liora" Hebrew for "I have light" speaks to the quality we seek in every piece: something that illuminates a room, catches the eye, and anchors the soul.

Our Beliefs

Design Philosophy

Material Honesty

We let our materials speak. No veneers, no faux finishes. Every grain, every vein of marble, every bubble in the glass is celebrated, never concealed.

Purposeful Minimalism

We remove everything that does not need to be there. Every edge, every proportion, every join is considered. The result is furniture that feels inevitable.

Community of Craft

We work with a family of skilled Kenyan artisans who have honed their craft over decades. Every Casa Liora piece supports local livelihoods and traditional skills.

How We Work

The Making
of a Piece

01

Material Selection

We source only the finest materials — visiting marble quarries in Italy and selecting oak logs with the most expressive grain patterns.

02

Design & Prototyping

Each piece is designed by hand, then translated into full-scale prototypes. We iterate until the form feels completely natural.

03

Hand Finishing

Our artisans spend up to 40 hours sanding, oiling, and inspecting every surface. No shortcuts. No machines for the finishing work.

04

Quality & Delivery

Before leaving the studio, every piece is photographed, documented, and white-glove packed for delivery to your home.

Our Commitment

Built to Last,
Made Responsibly

We believe the most sustainable piece of furniture is one you never need to replace. That is why we build for permanence, using materials that age beautifully and joinery techniques that outlast trends. We source our oak from FSC-certified forests and our marble from quarries with responsible extraction practices.

FSC Certified Wood

All timber is sourced from responsibly managed forests.

Zero-Waste Workshop

Wood off-cuts become smaller decor pieces or workshop fuel.

Local Employment

100% of production happens in our Karen, Nairobi workshop.
